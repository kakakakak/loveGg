<td class="answercell">
    <div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>According to <em>Hadoop: The Definitive Guide</em>, "Spilled Records" counts the total number of records that were spilled to disk over the course of a job and includes both map and reduce side spills. It is possible for the "Spilled Records" count to be zero, and that is perfectly fine. In general, spilled records means you have exceeded the amount of memory available in the map output buffer. Having a small number of "Spilled Records" is generally not an issue. The settings for the available RAM are <code>io.sort.mb</code> and <code>io.sort.spill.percent</code> in your <code>mapred-site.xml</code>. If performance were an issue, you would want to tune these to minimize the spilled records. The presentation <a href="http://www.slideshare.net/cloudera/mr-perf" rel="nofollow">Optimizing MapReduce Job Performance</a> has more details, specifically slides #12 and #13. If you spill more than once, then you pay a 3x penalty in IO due to needing to merge the spills. If "Spilled Records" is more than "Map Output Records" + "Reduce Output Records" then you are doing more than one spill. Note that ultimately, the amount of RAM is limited by the heap size of your Java VM, so you may need to either increase your cluster size or increase the number of map tasks by increasing the input splits for a given job in order to reduce the number of spills.</p>

<p>In your specific example, "Spilled Records" is larger, so you are spilling more than once.</p>
