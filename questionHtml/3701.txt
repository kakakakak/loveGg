<td class="answercell">
    <div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>That's a good answer to yourself ! :)</p>

<p>3079 is the pid ( process id ) of namenode. See more details about jps's output here: <a href="http://docs.oracle.com/javase/7/docs/technotes/tools/share/jps.html" rel="nofollow">Jps doc</a>. BTW, you can verify the listening ports of namenode using the pid with a command such as:</p>

<pre><code>lsof -p 3079 -a -i
</code></pre>

<p>In your command output:</p>

<pre><code>java    3079 hduser   89u  IPv4  59998      0t0  TCP localhost:54310-&gt;localhost:46507 (ESTABLISHED)
</code></pre>

<p>That line indicates that other process established TCP connection with Namenode at port 46507. Possible processes could be resoure manager(RM) or data nodes etc. </p>
