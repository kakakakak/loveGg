<td class="answercell">
    <div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>With Hadoop 1.x and prior it is not possible. However, this is exactly what YARN is designed for. MapReduce 2.0 is built on top of YARN.</p>

<p>YARN is an abstraction for distributed resource management. Check it out <a href="http://hadoop.apache.org/docs/current/hadoop-yarn/hadoop-yarn-site/YARN.html" rel="nofollow">here</a></p>

<p><strong>EDIT</strong>: Actually I lied, in Hadoop 1.x and prior it is still somewhat possible to do non MapReduce jobs with some hackery. If you look at the way <a href="http://giraph.apache.org" rel="nofollow">Apache Giraph</a> was implemented, they hooked into the MapReduce framework to do task distribution. The Mapper just becomes a container for them to launch their real code. But in my humble opinion, that was not an elegant solution. Since YARN was released, there is also an implementation of Giraph on YARN which (again IMHO) is much more elegant.</p>
