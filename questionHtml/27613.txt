            <div style="display: block;" class="comment-body">
                <span class="comment-copy">Simply put, if you can figure out a way to do it with MapReduce, it is scaleable.  Over time you learn tricks, for instance how to do the equivalent of a database join by doing a MapReduce over the union of the sets, tagging the source, and having a key that maps joined things together.  Or that to make this truly scaleable you need to MapReduce from one distributed key/value store to another, possibly multiple times.  But ultimately this is an open ended question, and there is no simple answer.  Keep doing it and you learn how to do it better.</span>
