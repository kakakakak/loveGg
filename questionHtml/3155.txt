    <div class="post-text" itemprop="text">

<p>Is it safe to close my terminal or turn off my computer without killing my Hadoop job?</p>

<p>I am in the middle of a Hadoop job running via a cluster on Amazon's Elastic Map Reduce. I have my terminal open, where I am ssh'd into my master node. When I try to close my terminal, I get a warning that all processes will be killed. Will this kill my Hadoop job as well? Or can I power off my computer and ssh again later? Do I need a special tool (like tmux) to do this? </p>

<p>If it matters, I am using the default (<code>bin/bash</code>) terminal in Ubuntu 13.10. I started the job with a Pig script.</p>

<td class="answercell">
    <div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>No, you can happily kill the terminal . The mapreduce job is running on the EMR cluster and will continue to do so. You could come back , log into the master node and check <code>lynx http://localhost:9100 </code>for jotracker status anytime.<br> To kill a hadoop job you have to run the command <code>hadoop job -kill jobflowid </code>. <br>So, moot point is - at any time you can log into the jobtracker and see the status of your job.Also note that jobtracker's port address 9100 is default on EMR cluster. <br> Cheers.. </p>
