    <div class="post-text" itemprop="text">

<p>I am working with hadoop and need to change number of open files <code>ulimit -n</code>. I have seen similar questions on stackoverflow and elsewhere and have tried everything in those answers, but it still does not work. I am working with <code>ubuntu 12.04 LTS</code>. Here is what i have done:</p>

<p>change limits in <code>/etc/security/limits.conf</code>, i have put in settings for * and <code>root</code>. I have also changed  limits to some number like <code>10000</code> and <code>unlimited</code>. </p>

<pre><code>* soft nofile 1513687
* hard nofile 1513687
root soft nofile 1513687
root hard nofile 1513687
</code></pre>

<p>I have also tried above setting with <code>-</code> instead of <code>soft</code> and <code>hard</code>. After these changes, i have made changes to <code>/etc/pam.d/</code> files such as:</p>

<pre><code>common-session 
common-session-noninterative
login
cron
sshd
su
sudo
</code></pre>

<p>i have added <code>session required pam_limits.so</code> to the beginning of each file. I made a restart of the box in question and the settings did not take effect. </p>

<p>I have also found that there were files inside <code>/etc/security/limits.d/</code> directory for the users <code>hbase</code> <code>mapred</code> and <code>hdfs</code>. I have tried changing limits in these individual files as well to no avail. </p>

<p>I have tried putting <code>ulimit -S -n unlimited</code> inside <code>/etc/profile</code> as well. It did not work. </p>

<p>Finally, i have tried putting <code>limit nofile unlimited unlimited</code> inside <code>/etc/init.d/hadoop*</code> files as first line. Did not work. </p>

<p>One interesting thing though is, i do not have hbase installed on the box but i do have a <code>hbase.conf</code> file inside <code>/etc/security/limits.d/</code> directory. The settings in this file are reflected with <code>ulimit -n</code>. But settings from <code>hdfs.conf</code> and <code>mapred.conf</code> are not reflected. Which suggests that something is overwriting settings for <code>hdfs</code> and <code>mapred</code>. </p>

<p>I guess i have tried everything people suggested on several forums, is there anything else that i may have missed or done incorrectly? </p>

<p>I am using <code>CDH 4.4.0</code> as my hadoop distribution. </p>
