    <div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Hi~ I just read throw your code. I have same suggest as Gwen. If you want your result records with "Movie ID" + "Movie name" + "Actors". You must put all the output value into context.write() in the same time. So what Gwen suggested is what you have to do.</p>

<p>I think the job fail is not Mapreduce problem, but a HDFS one. Check out "<a href="http://stackoverflow.com/questions/21581448/hadoop-file-could-only-be-replicated-to-0-nodes-instead-of-1">Hadoop: File … could only be replicated to 0 nodes, instead of 1</a>".</p>

<p>One thing I'm curious is the JoinMap2 part.</p>

<pre><code>String values[] = line.toString().split("\t");
movieId = values[0].trim();
actorName = values[8].trim().replaceAll("\t", "actor Name");
</code></pre>

<p>You split the <strong><em>line</em></strong> with "\t", so it means that there must have no "\t" inside of any cell of <strong><em>values[]</em></strong>.</p>

<p>So you really want it to do at the 3rd line? to replace "\t" to "actor name"? There are no "\t" in <strong><em>values[8]</em></strong>.</p>

<p>You need at least do three things to complete your MapReduce job.</p>

<ol>
<li>Fix your HDFS.</li>
<li>Rewrite JoinMap2 to make sure it does output the answer you want. the actors.</li>
<li>Rewrite reducer, just as Gwen said.</li>
</ol>

    <div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Even though your code iterates through all the values, it doesn't seem to accumulate actor names, rather it keeps overriding the current actor name with new ones.<br>
Instead of this:</p>

<pre><code>actorName= splitVals[1] != null ? splitVals[1].trim() : "actorName";
</code></pre>

<p>Try this:</p>

<pre><code>actorName += splitVals[1] != null ? splitVals[1].trim() : "actorName" + ",";
</code></pre>
