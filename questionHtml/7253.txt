<p>I am trying to read a file in Spark Shell that comes with CentOS distribution of Cloudera on my local machine. Following are the commands I have entered in Spark Shell.</p>

<pre><code>spark-shell
val fileData = sc.textFile("hdfs://user/home/cloudera/cm_api.py");
fileData.count
</code></pre>

<p>I also tried this statment for reading file:</p>

<pre><code>val fileData = sc.textFile("user/home/cloudera/cm_api.py");
</code></pre>

<p>However I am getting</p>

<pre><code>org.apache.hadoop.mapred.InvalidInputException: Input path does not exist: hdfs://quickstart.cloudera:8020/user/cloudera/user/cloudera/cm_api.py
</code></pre>

<p>I haven't changed any settings or configurations. What am I doing wrong? </p>

<td class="answercell">
    <div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>You are missing the leading slash in your url, so the path is relative. To make it absolute, use</p>

<pre><code>val fileData = sc.textFile("hdfs:///user/home/cloudera/cm_api.py")
</code></pre>

<p>or</p>

<pre><code>val fileData = sc.textFile("/user/home/cloudera/cm_api.py")
</code></pre>

    <div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>In <code>"hdfs://user/home/cloudera/cm_api.py"</code>, you are missing the <code>hostname</code> of the URI. You should have pass something like <code>"hdfs://&lt;host&gt;:&lt;port&gt;/user/home/cloudera/cm_api.py"</code>, where <code>&lt;host&gt;</code> is Hadoop NameNode host and the <code>&lt;port&gt;</code> is, <em>well</em>, port number of Hadoop NameNode, which is <code>50070</code> by default.</p>
