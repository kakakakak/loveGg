<td class="answercell">
    <div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>In case you are using <code>Spring</code> framework in your application made with proprietary technology at your company, then you can take advantage of <code>Spring-Hadoop</code> support.</p>

<p>All you have to do is just add below configuration in your <code>applicationContext</code>:</p>

<pre><code>&lt;hdp:configuration&gt;
    fs.default.name=${fs.default.name.url}
    mapred.job.tracker=${mapred.job.tracker.url}
&lt;/hdp:configuration&gt;

&lt;hdp:hive-client-factory host="${hadoop.hive.host.url}" port="10000"
    xmlns="http://www.springframework.org/schema/hadoop" /&gt;

&lt;hdp:hive-template /&gt;
</code></pre>

<p>After that, autowire <a href="http://docs.spring.io/spring-hadoop/docs/snapshot/api/org/springframework/data/hadoop/hive/HiveTemplate.html" rel="nofollow"><code>HiveTemplate</code></a>,</p>

<pre><code>@Autowired
HiveTemplate hiveTemplate;
</code></pre>

<p>And then query Hive as shown below:</p>

<pre><code>List&lt;String&gt; list = hiveTemplate.query(queryString, parameterMap);
</code></pre>

<td class="answercell">
    <div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>DataNucleus apparently uses an OSGi-based plugin mechanism.  If you are not running it in an OSGi container and are just using a standard maven project, what's probably happening is the plugins are on the classpath but are not registered due to an issue with manifests.  You might try something like this:</p>

<pre><code>&lt;build&gt;
    &lt;plugins&gt;
        &lt;plugin&gt;
            &lt;groupId&gt;org.apache.maven.plugins&lt;/groupId&gt;
            &lt;artifactId&gt;maven-dependency-plugin&lt;/artifactId&gt;
            &lt;version&gt;2.4&lt;/version&gt;
            &lt;executions&gt;
                &lt;execution&gt;
                    &lt;id&gt;copy-dependencies&lt;/id&gt;
                    &lt;phase&gt;package&lt;/phase&gt;
                    &lt;goals&gt;
                        &lt;goal&gt;copy-dependencies&lt;/goal&gt;
                    &lt;/goals&gt;
                    &lt;configuration&gt;
                        &lt;outputDirectory&gt;${project.build.directory}/jars&lt;/outputDirectory&gt;
                        &lt;overWriteReleases&gt;false&lt;/overWriteReleases&gt;
                        &lt;overWriteSnapshots&gt;false&lt;/overWriteSnapshots&gt;
                        &lt;overWriteIfNewer&gt;true&lt;/overWriteIfNewer&gt;
                    &lt;/configuration&gt;
                &lt;/execution&gt;
            &lt;/executions&gt;
        &lt;/plugin&gt;
    &lt;/plugins&gt;
&lt;/build&gt;
</code></pre>

<p>This was <a href="http://stackoverflow.com/questions/10096772/datanucleus-jdo-and-executable-jar-how-to-do-it">answered previously</a>.</p>
