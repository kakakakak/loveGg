<td class="answercell">
    <div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>The good news is that the exact sequence of steps that you provided works correctly on a fresh install of a single node DSE 4.5.1 cluster.</p>

<p>The logs don't indicate any problem other than a "UnavailableException", which means that the query is making it to Cassandra, and Cassandra doesn't think that enough nodes are up fulfill your request.</p>

<p>With RF=1, the implication is that if any node in your cluster is down, then you will hit that exception for some portion of your data set.</p>

<p>My reccomendation would be to </p>

<ol>
<li>Try this again on a single node with DSE 4.5.1 installed.</li>
<li>Assuming that works, double check the topology and status of your cluster "nodetool ring", or opscenter's ring view would both be quite helpful for this.</li>
</ol>

<p>The Cassandra logs should also show this UnavailableEXception, and possibly point you more directly at the underlying source of the problem.</p>

<p>I don't see any indication that this problem is specific to the configuration or use of Pig itself.</p>
