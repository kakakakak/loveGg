<td class="answercell">
    <div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Actually stdout only shows the <code>System.out.println()</code> of the <strong>non-map reduce</strong> classes. </p>

<p>The <code>System.out.println()</code> for map and reduce phases can be seen in the logs. Easy way to access the logs is </p>

<p><em><a href="http://localhost:50030/jobtracker.jsp-" rel="nofollow">http://localhost:50030/jobtracker.jsp-</a>>click on the completed job->click on map or reduce task->click on tasknumber->task logs->stdout logs.</em></p>

<p>Hope this helps</p>

<td class="answercell">
    <div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Another way is through the terminal:</p>

<p><b>1)</b> Go into your <em>Hadoop_Installtion</em> directory, then into <em> "logs/userlogs"</em>.<br>
<b>2)</b> Open your <em>job_id</em> directory.<br>
<b>3)</b> Check directories with <em>_<em>m_</em></em> if you want the mapper output or <em><em></em>_r_ </em> if you're looking for reducers. </p>

<p><b>Example</b>: In Hadoop-20.2.0:</p>

<pre>
> ls ~/hadoop-0.20.2/logs/userlogs/attempt_201209031127_0002_m_000000_0/
log.index   stderr      stdout      syslog
</pre>

<p>The above means:<br>
Hadoop_Installation: ~/hadoop-0.20.2<br>
job_id: job_201209031127_0002 <br>
_m_: map task , "map number": _000000_ <br></p>

<p><b>4)</b> open <em>stdout</em> if you used "system.out.println" or <em>stderr</em> if you used "system.err.append".</p>

<p>PS. other hadoop versions might have a sight different hierarchy but they're all should be under $Hadoop_Installtion/logs/userlogs. </p>
