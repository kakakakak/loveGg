<td class="answercell">
    <div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>I've had to do this a number of times so I came up with the following command line that you can throw in a script somewhere and reuse.  It prints the jobid followed by the job name.</p>

<pre><code>hadoop job -list | egrep '^job' | awk '{print $1}' | xargs -n 1 -I {} sh -c "hadoop job -status {} | egrep '^tracking' | awk '{print \$3}'" | xargs -n 1 -I{} sh -c "echo -n {} | sed 's/.*jobid=//'; echo -n ' ';curl -s -XGET {} | grep 'Job Name' | sed 's/.* //' | sed 's/&lt;br&gt;//'"
</code></pre>

<td class="answercell">
    <div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>If you do <code>$HADOOP_HOME/bin/hadoop -job -status &lt;jobid&gt;</code> you will get a <em>tracking URL</em> in the output. Going to that URL will give you the tracking page, which has the name</p>

<pre><code>Job Name: &lt;job name here&gt;
</code></pre>

<p>The <code>-status</code> command also gives a file, which can also be seen from the <em>tracking URL</em>. In this file is a <code>mapred.job.name</code> which has the job name.</p>

<p>I didn't find a way to access the job name from the command line. Not to say there isn't... but not found by me. :)</p>

<p>The <em>tracking URL</em> and xml <em>file</em> are probably your best options for getting the job name. </p>
