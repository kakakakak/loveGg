    <div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>You are asking several questions, and while they are related, they have different answers. I am answering them one-by-one below.</p>

<blockquote>
  <p>Is it normal having to wait so long between a map phase and a reduce phase with this data size?</p>
</blockquote>

<p>There's a barrier between the map and reduce phases. Your reducers cannot start until all your mappers finish. You have some mappers that are failing, thus slowing up your whole map phase and blocking the reduce phase. Once you solve that problem, your reduce phase should start earlier.</p>

<p>Why do your map tasks fail? Apparently they are not reporting progress:</p>

<pre><code>[...] failed to report status for 1225 seconds. Killing!
</code></pre>

<blockquote>
  <p>Is it possible that they the key-value pairs that i am emmiting from the mapper are causing this delay? Otherwise, why is this happening?</p>
</blockquote>

<p>I am not sure, but I did take a look at your code and you can make it run faster as follows:</p>

<p>1) Convert your <code>Text</code> to <code>IntWritable</code>; it looks like it is numerical data (a year), and doing this will reduce the amount of data being sent from mapper to reducers. See Tip 5 in this <a href="http://blog.cloudera.com/blog/2009/12/7-tips-for-improving-mapreduce-performance/" rel="nofollow">page on tips to improve Hadoop performance</a>.</p>

<p>2) Re-use your writables. You are creating a <code>new Text</code> on each iteration. You'd be surprised how inneficient this is, and leads to awful performance due to the continuous creation/de-allocation of objects in the heap. The idea is to create a writable once, and then re-use it. For details, see Tip 6 in this <a href="http://blog.cloudera.com/blog/2009/12/7-tips-for-improving-mapreduce-performance/" rel="nofollow">page on tips to improve Hadoop performance</a>.</p>

<p>While I cannot be sure of this, I suspect this may the the reason why some of your mappers are failing. Garbage collection can cause your program to pause while it completes, and thus not report progress, and as a result Hadoop kills the task.</p>

<p>3) If you are not already doing so, use more than one reducer. See Tip 3 in the page I linked above for some heuristics on how to set an appropriate number of map and reduce tasks for your job.</p>

<blockquote>
  <p>In any case, after all the 10 map tasks are completed (around map 100%, reduce 33%), the reducer takes nearly 2 hours to finish. How can this be possible given that it is an identity reducer?</p>
</blockquote>

<p>This can be normal behavior if you have too much data for a single (or a few) reducers. Shuffling means sorting at the reduce side. Try to sort a large file in a Linux box using <code>sort</code>. It can take a LONG time. This is what is happening in your job.</p>
