<td class="answercell">
    <div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>At the moment, every open <code>FSDataOutputStream</code> in the GCS connector for Hadoop consumes a thread until it's closed, because a separate thread needs to run the "resumable" HttpRequests while the user of the OutputStream writes bytes intermittently. In most cases, (such as in individual Hadoop tasks), there's only ever one long-lived output stream, and possibly a few shorter-lived ones for writing small metadata/marker files, etc.</p>

<p>In general, there are two possible causes for the OOM you're running into:</p>

<ol>
<li>You have lots of queued up jobs; every submitted job holds an unclosed OutputStream, and thus consumes a "waiting" thread. However, since you mention you only need to queue up ~10 jobs, this shouldn't be the root cause.</li>
<li>Something is causing a "leak" of the PrintWriter objects, originally created in <a href="http://grepcode.com/file/repo1.maven.org/maven2/com.ning/metrics.collector/1.2.1/org/apache/hadoop/mapred/JobHistory.java#1246" rel="nofollow">logSubmitted</a> and added to fileManager. Typically, terminal events (like <a href="http://grepcode.com/file/repo1.maven.org/maven2/com.ning/metrics.collector/1.2.1/org/apache/hadoop/mapred/JobHistory.java#1442" rel="nofollow">logFinished</a> will correctly close() all the PrintWriters before removing them from the map via <code>markCompleted</code>, but in theory they may be bugs here or there which can cause one of the OutputStreams to leak without being close()'d. For example, while I haven't had a chance to verify this assertion, it seems that IOException trying to do something like logMetaInfo will "removeWriter" <a href="http://grepcode.com/file/repo1.maven.org/maven2/com.ning/metrics.collector/1.2.1/org/apache/hadoop/mapred/JobHistory.java#1272" rel="nofollow">without closing it</a>.</li>
</ol>

<p>I've verified that at least under normal circumstances, the OutputStream seem to get closed correctly, and my sample JobTracker shows a clean jstack after having successfully run a lot of jobs.</p>

<p>TL;DR: There are some working theories as to why some resource may leak and ultimately prevent necessary threads from being created. You should consider changing <code>hadoop.job.history.user.location</code> to some HDFS location in the meantime, as a way to preserve the job logs in the absence of placing them on GCS.</p>
