<pre><code>hduser@ubuntu:/usr/local/hadoop$ bin/hadoop jar contrib/streaming/hadoop-*streaming*.jar -file /home/hduser/mapper.py -mapper /home/hduser/mapper.py -file /home/hduser/reducer.py -reducer /home/hduser/reducer.py -input /user/hduser/gutenberg/* -output /user/hduser/gutenberg-output
</code></pre>

<p>there it clearly shows there is no need to copy the mapper.py and reducer.py to the HDFS, you can link both the files from the local filesystem: as /path/to/mapper. I am sure you can avoid the above error. </p>

<td class="answercell">
    <div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>You might want to check that you don't have a dos-style new line after your #! line within mapper.py. If you do, hadoop may not be able to find your python interpreter since it'll see an extra CR. E.g. /usr/local/bin/python^M instead of /usr/local/bin/python where ^M is CR. Try dos2unix command on both your mapper and reducer.</p>
