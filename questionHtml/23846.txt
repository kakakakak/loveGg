<td class="answercell">
    <div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>1: No. 
2. Competitor to NoSQL: When you are building solutions, based on your requirements for a data-store it's NoSQL or our good old RDBMS's like SQL Server, MySQL, etc. 
3. Competitor to Hadoop: There are lot's of Big Data solutions out there. Hadoop (and it's various distributions) are leading but there are things like Massively Parallel processing databases, there's google's BigQuery, etc.. </p>

<p>Coming back to 1. Do not confuse Hadoop and NoSQL. Those are two different things - A NoSQL datastore can be part of a Hadoop Implementation. See "HBase". </p>

<p>I'll recommend you to start w/ a free ebook "planning for big data". It covers topics from high level but should get you started: </p>

<p><a href="http://parasdoshi.com/2012/05/18/want-to-learn-about-bigdata-read-oreillys-book-planning-for-bigdata/" rel="nofollow">http://parasdoshi.com/2012/05/18/want-to-learn-about-bigdata-read-oreillys-book-planning-for-bigdata/</a></p>

<p>It covers:
1. What is BigData?
2. What is Apache Hadoop?
3. Brief description of companies in BigData space
4. Microsoft’s plan for BigData
5. BigData in cloud
6. Data Market Places (like Azure DataMarket)
7. NoSQL World
8. Visualization
9. Future of BigData.</p>

<p>And pretty much answers all your questions. You can later decide to delve deeper if you wish but this is a good starting point. </p>
