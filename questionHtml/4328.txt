    <div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>I had a quick go at trying to reproduce this in the <a href="http://www.talend.com/solutions/big-data-sandbox" rel="nofollow">Talend Big Data Sandbox</a> but couldn't seem to get your error I'm afraid.</p>

<p>Plugging the error message into Google (with some variations) looks like this is a semi common error outside of Talend so I'd guess that as long as you are properly loading the necessary library and drivers and they are being included in your exported job then it's a configuration issue somewhere on your Hadoop cluster. It also seems to be happening on non MapR distros too.</p>

<p><a href="http://community.cloudera.com/t5/Storage-Random-Access-HDFS/org-apache-hadoop-hbase-client-NoServerForRegionException-Unable/td-p/9044" rel="nofollow">This issue</a> on the Cloudera community boards seems to have a satisfactory resolution where Oozie was misconfigured and was returning the same error as yourself. It might be worth trying to add:</p>

<pre><code>&lt;property&gt;
&lt;name&gt;oozie.credentials.credentialclasses&lt;/name&gt;
&lt;value&gt;hcat=org.apache.oozie.action.hadoop.HCatCredentials&lt;/value&gt;
&lt;/property&gt;
</code></pre>

<p>To <code>Oozie service-&gt;Configuration-&gt;Oozie Server(default)-&gt;Advanced-&gt; Oozie Server Configuration Safety Valve for oozie-site.xml</code> and restarting the Hive and Oozie services.</p>

<p>Of course that might be complicated by how your Hadoop cluster is administered and if you have a development cluster/local instance to run against that is also suffering from the same issue.</p>

<p>I'd strongly recommend installing the aforementioned Talend Big Data Sandbox or at least the <a href="https://www.mapr.com/products/mapr-sandbox-hadoop" rel="nofollow">MapR sandbox</a> if you only have a production or production like Hadoop cluster to deploy to.</p>
