<p>I am trying to capture image url's from inside tweets.</p>

<pre><code>REGISTER 'hdfs:///user/cloudera/elephant-bird-pig-4.1.jar';
REGISTER 'hdfs:///user/cloudera/elephant-bird-core-4.1.jar';
REGISTER 'hdfs:///user/cloudera/elephant-bird-hadoop-compat-4.1.jar';

--Load Json

loadJson = LOAD '/user/cloudera/tweetwall' USING com.twitter.elephantbird.pig.load.JsonLoader('-nestedLoad') AS (json:map []);
B = FOREACH loadJson GENERATE flatten(json#'tweets') as (m:map[]);
tweetText = FOREACH B GENERATE FLATTEN(m#'text') as (str:chararray);
</code></pre>

<p>intermediate date looks like this:</p>

<pre><code>(@somenameontwitter your nan makes me laugh with some of the things she comes out with like http://somepics.com/my.jpg)
</code></pre>

<p>then I try to do the following to get only the image url back :</p>

<pre><code> x = foreach tweetText generate REGEX_EXTRACT_ALL(str, '((http)(.*)(.jpg|.bmp|.png))');

dump x;
</code></pre>

<p>but that doesn't seem to work. I have also been trying with filter to no avail.</p>

<p>Even when trying the above with .* it returns empty results () or (())</p>

<p>I'm not good with regex and pretty new to Pig so it could be that I'm missing something simple here that I'm just not seeing.</p>

<p>I managed to get it working (though I doubt it is totally optimal)</p>

<pre><code>x = foreach tweetText generate REGEX_EXTRACT(str,'(http://.*(.jpg|.bmp|.png))',1) as image;


filtered = FILTER x BY $0 is not null;


dump filtered;
</code></pre>

<p>so the initial problem was just the regex (and my lack of knowledge on the subject).</p>
